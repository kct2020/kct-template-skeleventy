GoutPal Gout & Uric Acid Vocabulary

Keith Taylor June 26, 2020 May 18, 2020

Vocabulary entries link to the article they occurred in. Except where they occur in more than one article. In which case they link to the Vocabulary Tag for that term. Then Vocabulary Tags link to lists of articles that contain the term.

Acute to Chronic

acute gout

⇢ short-term gout pain – a flare or attack

adenosine

⇢ a type of purine

cardiovascular

⇢ heart and blood vessels

chronic gout

⇢ long-term uric acid arthritis

DECT to intra

DECT (Dual Energy Computed Tomography)

⇢ a non-invasive test that uses both the normal X-ray and also a second less powerful X-ray to make the images.

glucocorticoids

⇢ a class of steroid that is very effective at reducing inflammation and suppressing the immune system

guanine

⇢ a type of purine

hyperuricemia

⇢ high uric acid

hypoxanthine

⇢ a type of purine

intra-articular

⇢ within joints

Metabolism to Neurovascular

metabolism

⇢ chemical processes in our bodies that maintain life

MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)

⇢ a non-invasive test that uses strong magnetic fields and radio waves to produce detailed images of the inside of the body.

MSU (MonoSodium Urate)

⇢ uric acid (usually crystals) bonded with sodium and water molecules.

NaCl (Sodium Chloride)

⇢ common salt

neurovascular

⇢ nerves and blood vessels

Pathogenesis to sUA

pathogenesis

⇢ how a disease develops

periarticular

⇢ around joints

purine

⇢ compound in food providing savory taste (umami)

serum

⇢ liquid part of the blood

sUA (serum Uric Acid)

⇢ uric acid in the blood

Thiazide to Urate

thiazide diuretic

⇢ water pill – a type of drug that encourages passing urine

tophaceous

⇢ having, or related to lumps of uric acid crystals (see tophus)

tophus (plural = tophi)

⇢ lump of uric acid crystals

ULT (Urate Lowering Therapy)

⇢ uric acid treatment

ultrasound

⇢ a non-invasive test that uses high-frequency sound waves to create an image of part of the inside of the body

urate

⇢ uric acid (see MSU – monosodium urate)

Volar to XOI

volar surface

⇢ palm of the hand or sole of the foot

xanthine

⇢ a type of purine

XO or XOD (Xanthine Oxidase)

⇢ an enzyme that promotes uric acid production

XOI (Xanthine Oxidase Inhibitor)

⇢ uric acid blocker see Uric Acid Blockers
